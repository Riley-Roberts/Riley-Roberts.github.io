Experience in the Field

My professional journey spans from Google to Georgia Tech Research Institute, with an upcoming role at Deloitte. Through these experiences, I've developed expertise in software engineering, research, and technical leadership.

Deloitte, Summer 2025

I will be interning with Deloitte for the Summer of 2025 as a Cyber Analyst for Risk and Financial Advisory.

Georgia Tech Research Institute, Summer/Fall 2024

During my internship at Georgia Tech Research Institute, I focused on autonomous drone swarming and developing a reliable leader election system using a distributed computer algorithm. When the leader drone crashes, the swarm can fail, so I researched existing algorithms and designed a lightweight Min-Heap-based method to quickly elect a new leader using drone IDs. My solution kept the swarm functional in real time, even in GPS-denied or crash-prone environments.

Upon successfully presenting my findings and algorithm to my DoD team, GTRI offered to extend my internship to Fall '24.

Google, Summer 2023

During my STEP internship at Google, I explored low-level systems concepts by studying Scudo, the native memory allocator used in Android. I developed a tool to automatically verify configuration changes, ensuring they were implemented correctly and safely. I analyzed and documented complex interactions between configuration parameters to support this, making the codebase more understandable for future developers.

I also created targeted test cases to detect and prevent potential data leakage. I collaborated with engineers throughout the internship to deepen my knowledge of memory allocators, compilers, and Android's system architecture.

Alabama A&M University

At Alabama A&M, I've participated in multiple research projects. In my second year, I developed a Python-based machine learning model to predict brain activity from electrical signals, achieving 73% accuracy and presenting the results at University STEM Day.

In my third year, I joined a team researching advanced persistent threats and built a backend in Access and C# to display threat data and countermeasures. I also served as a teaching assistant for Computer Organization (CS 381) in the spring of my junior year.

Thurgood Marshall College Fund Pitch

Thurgood Marshall College Fund's annual pitch competition, The Pitch, brings together over 100 HBCU students to identify a problem and develop a solution in just three days. For my team, I built a custom website and led most of the pitch, covering everything except the financials.

Though it was my first pitch competition, I embraced the challenge, and the experience significantly boosted my confidence as I rose to the responsibility my team entrusted to me.

Alabama Collective Hackaton

I expanded my entrepreneurial experience at Alabama Collective's Innovate Alabama Hackathon, where we tackled STEM education for youth in underserved Alabama towns. As technical lead, I built a functional website using the ChatGPT API to generate personalized certification roadmaps through a user-friendly interface.

I led the solution section, demo, and Q&A during the pitch. We won third place.

Completed Projects

My portfolio showcases a range of projects spanning mobile app development, database design, and event planning. Each project demonstrates my technical ability and leadership skills.

Eventify Mobile Application

As team lead and front-end developer for Eventify, a mobile app built as part of my Senior Design course, I led our Agile-based workflow and coordinated development across the team. I developed the front-end using Xcode and Swift, implementing features like login authentication (including Face ID), account creation, and a search-enabled home page.

I also guided UI/UX design to ensure a clean, user-friendly experience. Eventify became my first venture into fully developing a front-end in coordination with the back-end, giving me a taste of full-stack development. We successfully presented our finished prototype, showcasing a fully functional and visually polished app that helps students easily discover campus events.

Database Projects

In my Database course, I designed and built the entire GreenGro Farms database from the ground up. The system tracks plants, employees, sales, inventory, and environmental data for a local gardening company. I created a fully normalized schema (3NF), wrote detailed SQL CREATE, INSERT, and SELECT statements, and implemented the project in Microsoft Access.

Database allowed me to utilize SQL, which I learned independently through coursework and hands-on practice. I successfully presented the project, demonstrating both the database structure and its practical functionality—earning praise for the quality and depth of my work.

During my research at Alabama A&M, I had the opportunity to build a backend using Access with a simplistic C# front end. Our backend served to organize our research and make it readily accessible to users.

SoilSense Mobile App

At the SoilSense pitch competition, I led software development, building a mobile app using JavaScript, CSS, and React (with Expo) within three days. The app serves as the user interface for our soil analysis device, delivering nutrient data and fertilizer recommendations, and offering an accessible platform for AI-driven insights tailored to small-scale farmers.

Additionally, I opened the presentation, introducing our project and setting the tone with a personal anecdote to highlight the urgency of soil degradation. The experience showcased my ability to lead under pressure, develop full-stack features quickly, and communicate a technical vision effectively.
